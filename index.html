In Barbieland, a Barbie doll faces expulsion because she doesn't conform to the traditional standards of perfection. Her unique eccentricity leads her on a journey into the real world. Upon her return to Barbieland to rescue it, she discovers that true perfection lies within and that self-belief is the key to happiness.

Greta Gerwig is an accomplished actress, playwright, screenwriter, and director. Known for her collaborations with Noah Baumbach and her solo directorial debut, "Lady Bird," Gerwig's career encompasses a range of critically acclaimed films. Gerwig's journey into filmmaking began with indie projects, and her breakthrough came with "Frances Ha" in 2012. She later wrote and directed the highly praised "Lady Bird" in 2017, solidifying her position as a prominent figure in contemporary cinema.

Margot Elise Robbie is an Australian actress known for her breakout role as Naomi Lapaglia in "The Wolf of Wall Street" (2013). She founded her production company, LuckyChap Entertainment, in 2014. Robbie gained acclaim for her portrayal of Tonya Harding in "I, Tonya" (2017) and played the iconic DC comics character Harley Quinn in "Suicide Squad" (2016).

Ryan Gosling began his career as a singer on "The Mickey Mouse Club" and transitioned to acting, gaining critical acclaim for his role in "The Believer" (2001). He achieved widespread recognition after starring in "The Notebook" (2004) and received an Academy Award nomination for "Half Nelson" (2006). Gosling has since become a celebrated actor known for roles in "La La Land" (2016), "Blade Runner 2049" (2017), and more, solidifying his place as one of his generation's finest talents.
